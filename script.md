# Roofus — Pitch Script

*Cox "Play With Purpose" Hackathon — Hapeville, GA*

---

## The problem

so hapeville has a food problem. 6 thousand people, packed with apartment complexes, and every single rooftop on top of them is just empty space.

## The smart move

now here's the smart part. i'm not trying to convince 6,000 people one by one to go build a greenhouse — that's never happening. i go to the apartment complex. one owner. one conversation. one deal, and the whole roof is in play.

and what most people don't realize is these owners are already in the power company's demand response program. so the greenhouse isn't a new pitch — it just plugs into something they're already getting paid for. they grow food on a roof that was doing nothing, and it makes their demand response deal even better.

that's the whole move — go where the deal is already easy, and where the rooftop's already worth money.

## What Roofus is

so that's what roofus is. it's a map of every apartment complex in hapeville, and every rooftop's already scored for you — so you know before you ever knock on a door which roofs are actually worth the conversation.

you click one and it just tells you straight up — is this roof big enough, does it get enough sun, can you even put a greenhouse up here. and then the part owners actually care about — does it make money. here's what you'd grow, here's what it costs, here's when you break even.

so instead of guessing, you walk into that one conversation already knowing the roof works and the numbers work.

## The demo

*[pull up the map — hapeville, every dot an apartment complex]*

ok let me just show you. this is hapeville — every dot is an apartment complex, and the greener it is, the better the rooftop.

*[click the biggest one — 2951 Macon Dr SW]*

this one right here — 2951 macon drive. one owner, 238 units, and look at that roof. almost 300 thousand square feet just sitting there empty. and these are the exact people living in the food desert — fresh food would be growing right on top of their own building.

roofus already did the math. it scores a 92 — the roof's flat, it's huge, it gets the sun. and the owner? they're already big enough that the power company pays them to dial back on hot summer days. they're already in the game.

so i'm not walking in asking them to start something new. i'm walking in saying — you've got 300 thousand feet of dead roof, you're already getting paid by the grid, let's put a greenhouse up here and make both of those worth more.

one click. one building. and i already know the roof works, the numbers work, and the owner's the easiest yes in the city.
