One representative summer day for a cluster — when Georgia Power calls a peak event, the cluster sheds its committed curtailable load (AC-cycling + greenhouse) while solar keeps feeding in, collapsing the evening grid draw. Illustrative.

How to read it: the red line is what the cluster pulls from the grid on a normal day; the green line is during a called CL-1 event. In the shaded window the green line drops far below the red — that gap is the committed curtailment, helped by the solar still flowing in daylight. That shed capacity is what CL-1 pays for.

Honest labels: sunlight is real (Open-Meteo); the curtailment shed is the cluster's real combined_curtailable_kw (AC-cycling + greenhouse), capped at the load actually present. Building load shape, battery rule, and the event window are modelled assumptions. Not a precise prediction.
